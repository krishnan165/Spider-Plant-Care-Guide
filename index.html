Spider Plant Care Guide

Soil for Spider Plant

Well-draining, light potting mix.

Spider Plants thrive in a well-draining potting mix; a lightweight mix with perlite is suitable for their growth.

Watering

Moderate watering, allowing the soil to partially dry between waterings.

Water the Spider Plant moderately, allowing the top layer of soil to dry before the next watering.

Lighting

Indirect, bright light.

Place Spider Plants in a location with indirect, bright light; they can tolerate lower light conditions but thrive in brighter settings.

Benefits

Air-purifying qualities.

Spider Plants are known for their air-purifying abilities, helping to remove common pollutants from indoor air.

Produces offsets or "pups."

Spider Plants produce offsets that can be propagated to grow new plants, making them great for sharing with others.

Non-Benefits

May be sensitive to overwatering; avoid waterlogged soil.
